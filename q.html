My Summer Reading List

They say that a reader will live a thousand lives before they die. As a lover of books, I believe this to be true. Every story can take you to a new place and a new adventure where you are surrounded by new people. Reading is my escape. It�s calming. As a college student, however, I find it difficult to find time to read for pleasure during the school year. Summers are my time to catch up on all the reading throughout the school year that I�ve missed out on. Every year, I head into summer with a list of books I want to read during my time off. It�s the perfect time to read. Summer is a time to read on a blanket in the sun or in a chair on the beach. Anywhere and everywhere, summer is my time to read. This summer, again, I have compiled a list of books I�ve been dying to read. Here it is.



The Crown by Kiera Cass



This is the last of the amazing Selection series. I have been in love with this since the beginning. The first book is entitled The Selection, so if you haven�t read them start there. This riveting story is a combination of The Hunger Games (without the killing) and The Bachelor. I won�t spoil too much, I promise. I leave in this short summary. The story follows Prince Maxon in search of a girl that will be the Queen to his King one day through a competition. The book is set in a future world where a caste system has returned. The series follows the competition, through the eyes of a hesitant and feisty contestant, America Singer. This is my favorite series I have ever read by far. I strongly encourage you to pick it up this summer.



The Chronicles of Narnia Series by C.S. Lewis



I know what most of you are thinking...this is a kid�s book or there�s no way you haven�t already read this. Well, for one, I have yet to read the whole series. For all the fans- I know, shame on me. Two, from what I�ve read of C.S. Lewis (which sadly isn�t that much) and what I�ve heard (which has been significantly more), he is a genius that can be read at any age with an infinite amount of knowledge to take away. I have heard so many good things about this series that I�m excited to finally see for myself. This series takes us to another world, Narnia, where adventure is around every corner. Check it out.



The Screwtape Letters by C.S. Lewis



Yes, C.S. Lewis and I will be spending a lot of time together this summer. This is a book that blows my mind in concept. It is about temptation and sin from the perspective of a tempter from the Devil himself. It gives us firsthand knowledge of ways that Satan tries to manipulate us and our emotions to disconnect us from our relationship with God so that we sin against him. This book gets me so excited because it�s literally like getting behind the scenes footage, the answer to a test. When we know how the Devil is fooling and using us, we are better equipped to avoid it. I�m thrilled to get the opportunity to go behind enemy lines this summer through this book. Join me.



Graceling by Kristin Cashore

�

This fantasy novel throws us into a world where people are selected and given a Grace, which is a talent of any sorts. Katsa, a strong and beautiful teenage girl, has been given the power to kill. She is the King�s niece, which means she must now also play hitman for him. With the Grace she�s been given, she now has the challenge of figuring out on her own how to best use it. Will she choose to use her power for good? Will there be anyone to help her along the way? I don�t know yet, but I plan to find out this summer. Will you?



Red Queen by Victoria Aveyard



This critically acclaimed novel combines the ideas of The Selection and Graceling. In this book we get put into a world where people are separated by blood- red blooded and silver blooded. The red blooded are commoners who are forced to serve the silver elite. The main character, Mare, is a red blooded thief who has abilities beyond the red blooded norm. To hide this fault in their society, the King covers it up by making Mare �the lost princess� and forcing her to marry one of his sons. Mare has some new ideas of her own, though, and she just might use this new found power and status to her benefit. Find out how in this exciting adventure.



The Kiss of Deception by Mary E. Pearson



Anyone enjoy love triangle stories? I certainly do. I love the debates they bring, #TeamX and #TeamZ. This novel has a classic love triangle with a twist. Princess Lia is being forced to marry a Prince from another kingdom to secure an alliance. Lia, however, is not about that idea at all. She flees the morning of the wedding and the shelter Princess begins to take residence in a distant village where she believes no one will find her. There she meets two handsome guys whom she gets close with, not knowing that one is the Prince she abandoned and another is an assassin sent to kill her. Who will she inevitably fall in love with? Who is Mr. Right? Get your team hashtags ready and dive into this book.



Me Before You by Jojo Moyes



Soon to be a major motion picture, this novel isn�t your cliche romance. Be prepared, it won�t be mushy or swooning. This is a story of two people, a real life story of their interactions and their love. After an accident made him wheelchair bound, Will is not able to do all the adventurous things he once could. He needs someone to take care of his well-off self. Louisa needs a job. She�s never really been a caregiver, but it�s a job and she needs one. Little does she know that this job and this person is going to change her ordinary life into something extraordinary. How exactly? You�ll have to read to find out.




A Separate Peace by John Knowles


Apparently this has been a classic, crazy awesome book for over thirty years now. I guess I�ve been living under a rock because I have just now come to hear about it. I can see why it has been a bestseller for so long, though. It looks incredible. Set in the early years of World War II, this is a story of two boys who (like everything during that time) go through a dark place that will change them both forever. Gene is a shy kid who is too smart for his own good, whereas Phineas is a handsome and sly dude. Their paths cross for the summer. A lot can happen in a summer, and some things you can�t take back. Find out what happens in this capturing novel.



Passengers by Alexandra Bracken



This is a complex story of time travel and life at sea. One moment can change everything. That�s what Etta Spencer found out on the night where she lost everything. She was thrown into a world she knew nothing about, not just miles but years from her home. Nicholas Carter loves his life at sea...that is, until the addition of a new passenger (Etta). Etta brings back part of Nicholas�s past that up until her he had been successfully escaping. They embark on a journey together to find a missing object that, in the wrong hands, may be dangerous. Their paths are now crossed whether they like it or not. To hear more about their adventure, check out this book this summer!





The Four Loves by C.S. Lewis


Didn�t I tell you Lewis and I would be seeing a lot of each other this summer? When I look at this brilliant mind�s work, I can�t help but be in awe. The way he thinks and writes challenges me. I get so much from it. This book I�ve heard referenced in a sermon. This book is all about Lewis describing the four different types of way in which we as humans love. Our love here on earth is a reflection of divine love. God is love and he has created us to love- love the Lord, our God and love our neighbors. See how Lewis explains the practicalities of living out God�s love in this �explanation of the nature of love.�

This is my reading bucket list for the summer. I've been waiting all year long to read this spectacular books. I encourage you to make your own summer reading list. I hope my list can spark yours. Take the time this summer and read all the things you've wanted to but just didn't feel you had the time to. Dive in. A thousand lives are waiting.
